Why pair programming is important?

Pair Programming is a development technique where two programmers work together on a workstation. Compared to the traidional solo programming approach, pair programming has many advantages.

It is believed that this produces the same amount of work as two separate programmers but with a higher quality. Further more, skills can also transferred between the two programmers, where the two have different experience and specialities. Ad-hoc training also occurs as one person shows the other some tricks, workarounds, etc.

Other benefits of pair programming include:

  • Collective code ownership - everbody gains a working knowledge of the entire codebase
  • Mentoring - Knowledge is transferred between the junior programmers or new team members when paired with a more experienced person on the team
  • Team cohesion - People get to know each other more quckly and learn how to work with each other
  • Improved morale - When done well, pair programming is mroe more enjoyable than programming alone
